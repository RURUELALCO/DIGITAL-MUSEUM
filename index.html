The Butterfly effect

Glimpse of who we are

Introduction

Imagine a museum without boundaries, where the artifacts come to life in the digital realm. The Butterfly effect is not just another museum; it's a clairvoyance. It's about peeking of who we are in the presents blending with the rich history of our past with the glimpse what we could be in the future. Let's dive into the essence of The Butterfly effect.

Technology

The emergence of social media platforms like Facebook, MySpace, and Friendster in the 2000s and 2010s drastically altered how Filipinos connected and communicated. Particularly in the Philippines, Facebook's rapid growth and dominance demonstrate the expanding power of social media. The rise of the "emo" and "scene" subcultures at this time, in addition to social media platforms, had a significant impact on fashion trends. The body positivity movement, which emphasizes acceptance and celebration of all body types and features, also gained momentum between 2000 and 2010. This cultural change implies an increase in the importance of diversity and accepting oneself.

Time

The Philippines' electoral process was modernized in 2008 with the introduction of the AES, which aimed to improve voting procedures' efficiency, accuracy, and transparency. The EDSA II protests, which were brought on by the public's displeasure over accusations of corruption against President Estrada, also took place during this time period. The relationship between gender, politics, and physical beauty was brought to light by the public's examination of female politicians. Arguments over their attire, haircuts, and physical characteristics highlighted the significance of appearance and presentation in politics and the media's major role in influencing public impressions of political leaders.

Self and Body Image

The rapid growth in urbanization and industrialization in the Philippines during the 2000s resulted in a major escalation in pollution levels within several urban areas. The possible consequences of decreased air quality and environmental deterioration may have an indirect influence on individuals' self-image, as increased anxiety and fear regarding the negative impact of pollution on their own health and physical appearance.
